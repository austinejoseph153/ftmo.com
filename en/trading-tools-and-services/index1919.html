Equity Simulator

Understanding the dynamics of equity curves is key in navigating the uncertainties of trading. Drawdowns are inevitable, and experiencing them is part of the journey. Consider this: if your strategy typically encounters a 20% drawdown and you currently find yourself at 11%, you’re still well within your anticipated range.

The Equity Simulator serves a strategic purpose. It’s not about fortune-telling your financial future; instead, it’s a tool designed to comprehensively simulate potential outcomes of your trading method. The objective here is mental readiness for any scenario the market may present. It’s a disciplined approach to risk management and strategic planning.

Find out more about the crucial topic of risk management, luck and probability factors in trading.

Margin Calculator

Our FTMO margin calculator allows you to calculate the size of the margin required and the value of the position on your chosen symbol and in the currency of your trading account.

The calculation takes into account the current market rate of the symbol (which can be changed if necessary) and the volume in lots. The calculation can also take into account the account type (Normal, Swing) and you can set your individual leverage. The calculation will show you the required margin and the value of the position in the currency of your account, and also in the currencies comprising the currency pair, or in the base currency in which the symbol is traded. In addition, you will also learn the size of the maximum position you can open on a given symbol at a given account size. More about margin calculator and its role in trading.

Pip Calculator

Our FTMO pip calculator will show you the nominal value of a pip on your chosen symbol and in the currency of your trading account.

The calculation takes into account the current market rate and the volume you plan to trade (lots). Knowing the value of a pip is essential when preparing a trade, especially when you need to calculate the value of the trade outcome and its relationship to predefined risk management and stop loss parameters in your account. The calculation is based on the bid price (bid price). More about pip calculator  on the topic of pips, ticks and points.

Time Zone Converter

In our Trading Objectives, the business day is defined according to Central European Time – CE(S)T – [Central European (Summer) Time]. This time may not always be the same as the server settings and therefore the time you see in the platform. Compliance with the rules is always monitored according to the time zone CE(S)T.

As a result, we have created a time zone converter that will show you all the important current times so you know when your maximum daily loss is reset, for example.
